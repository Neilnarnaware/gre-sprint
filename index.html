      The test, briefly

      The GRE General Test runs about 1 hour 58 minutes with no scheduled break. Three measures:

      • Verbal Reasoning — two sections, 27 questions total. Scored 130–170.
      • Quantitative Reasoning — two sections, 27 questions total. Scored 130–170.
      • Analytical Writing — one "Analyze an Issue" essay, 30 minutes. Scored 0–6.

      It's section-adaptive: performance on the first Verbal/Quant section sets the difficulty of the second. Scores are valid for 5 years.

      4

      Smartest shortcuts

      • Vocab is the cheapest points in Verbal. 15 words/day × 30 = 450 words. Do them in dead time — commute, queues, kettle boiling.
      • Learn the on-screen calculator early, and its limits (basic operations only).
      • Quant is mostly high-school math tested cleverly: percentages, ratios, exponents, geometry, statistics, word problems.
      • Plug in numbers on variable questions; work backwards from answer choices when stuck.
      • For the essay, memorize one flexible 4-paragraph template. Practice 3–4 prompts, not 30.
      • Take 1 full test early (diagnostic) and 1 in the final week. ETS PowerPrep mirrors the real interface.

      Quant cheat-sheet

      • Percent change = (new − old)/old × 100
      • Average = sum ÷ count · Speed = distance ÷ time
      • Exponents: aᵐ·aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ ; (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ ; a⁰ = 1
      • Special triangles: 3-4-5, 5-12-13 ; 45-45-90 → 1:1:√2 ; 30-60-90 → 1:√3:2
      • Circle: area πr², circumference 2πr
      • Probability = favorable ÷ total ; OR adds, AND multiplies
      • Quadratic: x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a
